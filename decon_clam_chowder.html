Deconstructed Clam Chowder

Recipe Description

Potatoes are sliced and parcooked, then layered with a cream sauce made with bacon, clams, and onion. The Casserole is then topped with a butter and bread crumb topping and baked until bubbling and golden brown on top.

Recipe makes one 6 x 6 x 2 inch casserole

Ingredients

  • 2 Large Russet Potatoes, washed and sliced into 1/4" slices
  • For Clam Sauce
    • 1 12oz Can Evaporated Milk
    • 1.5 T Butter
    • 2 T Flour
    • 1 Medium Onion, Minced
    • 2 Strips Bacon
    • 1 6.5oz can Bar Harbor Brand Chopped or Minced Clams
  • For Crumb Topping
    • 2 T butter
    • 1 C Bread Crumbs

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 350 F
  2. Bring one large pot of salted water to a boil
  3. Fry 2 strips bacon in a seperate sauce pot
  4. Cook potatoes in boiling water for 3 minutes, rinse with cold water and drain
  5. When bacon is cooked, remove from sauce pot, add onion a and 1.5 T butter
  6. Cook onion until soft, and 2T flour and whisk into a roux
  7. Cook roux for two minutes
  8. Slowly add milk and reserved clam juice while whisking to make sauce
  9. Add clams and remove from heat
  10. Layer potatoes and clam sauce into casserole
  11. Melt 2 T butter in a small pan and toss with breadcrumbs to coat
  12. Layer breadcrumbs on top of casserole
  13. Bake for 25 minutes or until bubbling and golden brown on top
